Introduction

Games are making kids better problem-solvers, and will make us better at everything from driving to multi-tasking.

Zicherman, 2011

Math Farm is a gamified interactive math game built based on the presense of key game mechanics such Points, Badges, Leaderboards (PBLs), and instant feedback.

Math Farm provides many activities to help kids develop an active attitude towards study in general, and math in particular, and to achieve a particualr learning goal designed by gamification designer while playing the activties.

Gamification has the power to transform the way we teach and the way we learn.

Matera, 2015

Gamification is not only a simple set of techniques and mechanics, but also “an alternative framework for training and shaping participant behaviour that has at its core the concepts of entertainment and engagement” (Tulloch, 2014). The reason gamification can match with pedagogy is that there are some similarities between games and study. The structure of similarities (inherited from Herger, 2014) below shows how Math Farm uses gamification techniques to develop study activities that provide gameful experience.

Table 1: Comparing Game and Study
Game Elements Study Game
Tasks Repetitive, and boring Repetitive, but fun
Feedback Depend on teacher’s availability Constantly and (mostly) instantly
Path to Mastery Unclear Clear
Rules Unclear and nontransparent Clear and transparent
Information Too much and not enough, not easy to understand Right amount at the right time. Clear and portable instructions
Failure Forbidden, punished, better to prevent it at all time Expected, encouraged, spectacular, brag about it
Status of Users Mostly appear at the end of a semester or a year Transparent, timely
Promotion Meritocracy but less insight how and when a promotion happens Meritocracy
Collaboration Yes but lack of choices Yes and freedom of choices
Narrative If we agree that syllabus is a kind of narrative then it is a boring story Interactive and engaged stories
Obstacles Accidental On purpose as designed as challenges

Gameplay

Players begin with an empty farm and a fixed starting number of coins which are the primary currency in the game.

Players move around the environment and attack enemies to collect coins and experience points (XP). An attack is considered successful when players give a correct answer to a math question. When an attack is successful, the player earn coins and XP described in enemy’s detail. There is no penalty for wrong answer. If a player is unable to answer a question, they can use related instructions as a hint to solve the question without penalty.

XP is used for level up. When players reach a certain level, they will choose a fixed title from the title list. If a player owns a title, the gamification master must call the player by their title instead of their real name. Players will be ranked in a leader board based on their XP. Players also achieve badges when they complete some specific tasks. Badges may reward coins and XP.

Players can use coins to buy items which are images of houses, decorations, trees, pets, etc. to design their farm. The best farm will be selected by a vote from all participants at the end of the semester. The winner will be listed in the Hall of Fame. Coins are also used to exchange action cards. The person who owns an action card can request another person to perform the action described in the card. Cards may or may not have rewards. Furthermore, players can exchange coins for a custom title. The cost for custom title depends on its length followed by the list below;

Table 2: Custom title exchange rate
Length Cost Rate
2 word title 80 40 coins/word
3 word title 90 30 coins/word
4 word title 100 25 coins/word
5 word title 110 22 coins/word

As the activity progresses, the difficulty of questions will be increased based on players’ level and skills. When the difficulty increases, the rewards increase as well. At the final session, time is required to answer a question.

Different players may have different goals when they play. The list below is the possible goals from this activity:

  • Buy items for decorating player’s farm
  • Collect badges/titles
  • Buy action cards to interact with other players
  • Maintain high rank in leader board (based on XP)
  • Save coins as much as possible
